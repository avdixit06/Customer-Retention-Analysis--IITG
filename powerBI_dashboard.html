Analytical Findings & Strategic Signals

✅ EBL Definition 1 Validated

Engagement-Based Loyalty produces a stronger repeat-purchase gap between loyal and non-loyal segments than the Spend-Sentiment definition. Core Loyalists average significantly higher previous purchases with zero discount activation — confirming intrinsic motivation.

⚠ Promo-Dependent Segment: Highest Risk

Promo-Dependent customers show the highest avg repeat purchases (~41.1) of any segment but are 100% discount-reliant. They are deeply engaged but entirely price-driven. Reducing discounts here risks the highest-frequency buyers — requires a graduated exit strategy.

📍 BAI Identifies Underserved Markets

Kansas (BAI ~0.641, disc 23.8%) and Alaska (BAI ~0.635) show strong organic pull relative to their customer count. These markets are under-marketed — genuine brand affinity exists without promotional stimulus, making them cost-efficient acquisition targets.

🛍 Accessories = Retention Anchor

Accessories attracts customers with the highest avg previous purchases (~25.7), placing it squarely in the Veteran Purchase_History_Band. Outerwear skews toward Emerging buyers with highest discount rate (~44.4%) — it is a category entry door, not a loyalty driver.
